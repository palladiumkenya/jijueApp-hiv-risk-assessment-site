WHAT DOES IT MEAN TO BE LOW RISK?

This means that you are less likely to have been exposed to HIV based on the risk assessment.

To confirm your HIV status, you can get tested by:

Booking an appointment to visit the nearest VCT centre or
order a self-test kit and test yourself at the comfort of your home.


You can remain at low risk by;

- Practicing safe sex by consistent and correct condom use.

- Do not use an already used needle/syringe

- Frequent screening for sexually transmitted infections .

- Continually educate yourself on HIV transmission.


All these will reduce the chances of getting exposed to HIV.

WHAT DOES IT MEAN TO BE MEDIUM RISK?

This means that you are moderately likely to have been exposed to HIV.

However, this does not mean that you will definitely turn out to be HIV positive once tested.

Booking an appointment to visit the nearest VCT centre or
